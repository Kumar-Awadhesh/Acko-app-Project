LIFE INSURANCE

Life insurance policy is a contract-based promise established between the policyholder, who is the insured individual, and the insurer. Under this
agreement, the policyholder must pay the insurer a fixed amount (premium payments). In exchange for these premiums, the insurer commits to
providing a sum of money, known as the death benefit, to the beneficiary chosen by the policyholder in case of the policyholder's demise. Life
insurance comes in different types, and some plans also provide the maturity benefit if the policyholder outlives the policy term.
